Methods of Adoption:

  1. Domestic adoption: Adopting a child within one’s own country.

    2. Domestic adoption refers to the situation where a child who is resident in Ireland is adopted by a person or couple who is/are also resident in Ireland. The nature and effect of an Irish adoption order is that the child becomes the child of the adopter(s) as
    if born to them, with all the rights and duties of parent(s) and children in relation to each other.
    Please note that Irish adoption legislation only allows for the adoption of a child. A child is defined as a person who has not yet reached eighteen years of age. It is not possible for a person eighteen years of age or older to be adopted in Ireland.
    There are four different types of domestic adoption – stepfamily, extended family/relative adoption, domestic infant adoption and long term foster care to adoption.


  2. International adoption: Adopting a child from another country.
  3. Open adoption: Maintaining some level of contact with the birth parents.
  4. Closed adoption: No contact between adoptive and birth families.
  5. Kinship adoption: When a relative adopts a child (e.g., grandparents, aunts, uncles).
